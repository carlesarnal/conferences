We merged 2,000 PRs last year with a core team of five engineers spread across four time zones. Some months felt unstoppable; others, a single mass-unsubscribe from a mailing list nearly stalled a release. This is what maintaining Apicurio — now a CNCF sandbox project — actually looks like behind the commit log.

In this talk, I'll share hard-won lessons from years as a Principal Software Engineer and maintainer in the Apicurio community. No platitudes — concrete stories about what worked and what failed:

- How we turned a contributor's frustrated GitHub issue into a sustained three-year partnership
- The async communication playbook that kept a global team aligned without meeting fatigue
- A governance decision that split the community — and how we recovered
- The sustainability model we adopted after nearly burning out the core team

Whether you maintain a project with three contributors or three hundred, you'll leave with actionable strategies for engagement, conflict resolution, and long-term project health — grounded in real examples, not theory.
